## Requirements
Future wireless communication systems (5G and 6G) must provide:
1.Low Latency
2.High data throughput
3.Low jitter
4.High mobility
5.High Quality of Service (QoS)

## High Data Throughput
Throughput is the actual amount of data successfully transmitted per second.

Example:

If your internet downloads a movie at 200 Mbps, then

Throughput = 200 Mbps

## Low Latency
Latency is the time taken for data to travel from the transmitter to the receiver.

## Low Jitter
Jitter is the variation in latency.
Example:

Packet 1 → 10 ms

Packet 2 → 11 ms

Packet 3 → 9 ms

Very stable.

Now another case:

Packet 1 → 10 ms

Packet 2 → 80 ms

Packet 3 → 15 ms

The delay keeps changing.

This variation is called jitter.

High jitter causes:

Voice distortion
Video freezing
Poor gaming experience

## High Mobility
Users continue moving while communicating.
The network should maintain the connection even when users move quickly.

## Quality of Service (QoS)
QoS means maintaining communication quality according to the application's needs.
For example:
1.Application ->	Requirement
2.Voice call ->	Low delay
3.Video streaming -> High throughput
4.Remote surgery	-> Very low delay + High reliability
5.Gaming	-> Low latency + Low jitter
